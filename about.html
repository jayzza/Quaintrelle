The Optimist


       I'm a great loser and I fail plenty of times already. There may be moments that I'm cynical, however; I know in myself that I can do things no matter what is or how hard it is as long as I believe in myself and I remain engaged. I'm a strong and independent person and I proved it as I'm apparently living far from home to study and be able to fulfil my dreams. I've accomplished things with careful perseverance. I'm hard-working and I can associate this cardinal disposition to my goal oriented mind. I focus on my goals. I think big. I plan things so it will work out for me and I always weigh things; the risks and benefits. I take calculated risks. I visualize both positive and negative outcomes in order to identify what are the actions needed to be done. On the other hand, from all the hard works I reward myself by resting or making time to relax and do things that excites me.

       I believe I'm diligent and I choose empowering beliefs. I get my priorities straight. A great loser is what I am sometimes but I'm hard-working so I fail with grace. And most importantly, I'm willing to fail even more because with failure comes learning.
